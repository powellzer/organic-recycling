Let's make composting easy

By creating a network of Community-Run Compost Bins, We can make Organic Recycling easier and create closer environmentally friendly communities

Our mission is simple - composting should be easy. We want to help people cut down on organic waste by creating a well-organized and accessible network of community compost bins run by members of the community.

Here's how it works:

  • Compost bins are set up and maintained by CityCompost Guardians
  • Members of the community can contribute organic waste to the bins
  • Commercial partners empty the bins and generate compost instead of the same waste ending up in landfills

Why Recycle Organic Waste?

Every year billions of tons of food scraps placed in the garbage are carried to landfills to decompose and produce methane gas, a greenhouse gas which is far more potent that carbon dioxide.
Composting is natureâ€™s way of recycling. It is one of the most powerful actions we can take to reduce our trash, address climate change, and build healthy soil. By turning our food scraps and yard trim into compost, we can transform our waste streams into a beneficial, value-added soil amendment and use it to protect the environment and create resilient communities.

Food Waste List

Food waste that can be organically recycled includes: coffee grounds and filters, eggs/eggshells, dairy products, fruits and veggies, grains (bread, pasta, rice, etc), shells from seafood, and tea.

Coffee & Tea

You can include used coffee grounds, paper coffee filters and tea bags!

Eggs & Egg Shells

Cooked eggs are great to compost and egg shells provide the compost a great source of minerals.

Dairy Products

Spoiled milk, old yogurt, left-over cearal milk, and moldy cheese all make great additions to your compost.

Fruits & Veggies

Fruits and veggies quickly break down and provide the compost a great source of nitrogen.

Seafood Shells

Clean up your low country boil right into your Compost bin and make our soil happy.

Breads & Grains

Stale and moldy bread makes for great compost. Leftover pasta and rice can also be added to the bin.
